From Ann Arbor to Houston

Duration: 14 days
Date: 2018.12.21 - 2019.01.04
Route: Ann Arbor - Nashville - New Orleans - Houston - Austin - Dallas - St. Louis - Ann Arbor
Milliage: 3271 miles

We really wanted to be in somewhere that is nice and warm during winter break again this summer, which narrow down some choice of destinations. We've driven to Key West last summer, and was in Mexico the summer before last summer, and we kinda want to eat a lot. So, New Orleans' seafood and Texas' BBQ became our top choice.
We booked our first night hotel, and that was all the thing we planned. This time, we decided not to plan anything ahead, so we had more freedom adjusting our trip along the way.

First Day

Ann Arbor - Louisville
Time: 6 hours
Milliage: 350 miles

We were planning to leave Ann Arbor before 12 pm, but we actually did not get to leave until 2 pm because we really wanted to eat ramen in Lan City in Ypsilanti.

We decided to stop at Le Moo, a steakhouse at Louisville. It's a dimly lit, industrial style dinning room with a lot of decoration on the wall. Steak was good, but a little bit too salty. The most interesting thing in this restaurant was their check holder (a book). We spent a lot of time reading the book while waiting for our server, and we found a lot of old check from previous customers! It was a fun experience. (and kinda expansive experience for us poor students)

Since we planned to visit Mammoth Cave National Park the next day, we just picked a random motel near the park and stay for a night.
